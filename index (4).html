The System
People don't open YouTube for music. They open it to change their state — Focus. Calm. Energy. You're not building a music channel. You're building an environment delivery system. Here's how it works:

Execution Steps
Step 01
Define your niche inside the niche
Don't just pick "Lo-Fi." Pick "Lo-Fi for people who study late at night." Don't just pick "Focus Music." Pick "Deep Focus for trading / coding / writing." The more specific your identity, the faster your algorithm placement. YouTube needs to know exactly who to show your video to. Broad channels confuse the algorithm. Niche channels train it.
"Lo-Fi Study Music" has 10,000 competitors. "Lo-Fi for Night Owl Coders" has 50. Same content, 200x less competition."
Step 02
Generate and filter your audio ruthlessly
Use Suno or Udio. Generate at least 20 versions per session. Keep 3. Delete the rest. Most people publish whatever comes out first. That's the mistake. Selection is the skill — not generation. Your filter criteria: Does it produce the state? Is it consistent for 30+ minutes without annoying loops? Does it sound like you paid for it?
"The tool is not the product. The judgment of what to keep is the product."
Step 03
Build visuals that create identity alignment
Your thumbnail and visual aren't decoration — they're the promise. A clean desk says "this is for productive people." A trader's screen says "this is for people who make decisions under pressure." Your viewer should look at your visual and see who they want to be. That's what makes them stay.
Step 04
Optimise for session time, not views
One video drives most traffic. The rest of your channel converts that traffic into subscribers and session time. Build playlists immediately. Name them around outcomes: "4-Hour Deep Work Sessions" not "Lo-Fi Playlist #3". YouTube's algorithm rewards channels that keep people on YouTube. Long videos + playlists = exactly that.
